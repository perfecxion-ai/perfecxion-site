## Part I: Neural Computation Fundamentals
### The Neuron: Building Block of Intelligence
At its core, artificial intelligence begins with a simple computational unit inspired by biological neurons. Understanding this foundation is essential for grasping why certain vulnerabilities exist at a mechanistic level.
#### The Perceptron's Three-Stage Process

<div class="bg-gray-100 dark:bg-gray-900 rounded-lg p-6 mb-6">
<h4 class="font-bold mb-4">The Artificial Neuron</h4>

<span class="text-blue-600 font-bold">1</span>
</div>

### The Transformer Architecture Revolution
The 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need" marked a paradigm shift from sequential processing to parallel attention mechanisms, enabling the massive scale of modern LLMs.
#### Self-Attention Mechanism

<div class="bg-gray-100 dark:bg-gray-900 rounded-lg p-6 mb-6">
<h4 class="font-bold mb-4 flex items-center gap-2">
<Eye class="h-5 w-5 text-primary-500" />
How Self-Attention Works
</h4>

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="bg-white dark:bg-gray-800 p-4 rounded-lg border-l-4 border-blue-500">
<h5 class="font-semibold mb-2">Query (Q): "What am I looking for?"</h5>

<div class="text-sm text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-400">Each token asks what information it needs</div>

</div>

<div class="bg-white dark:bg-gray-800 p-4 rounded-lg border-l-4 border-green-500">
<h5 class="font-semibold mb-2">Key (K): "What information do I offer?"</h5>

<div class="text-sm text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-400">Each token advertises its available information</div>

</div>

<div class="bg-white dark:bg-gray-800 p-4 rounded-lg border-l-4 border-purple-500">
<h5 class="font-semibold mb-2">Value (V): "My actual content"</h5>

<div class="text-sm text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-400">The information content to be retrieved</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-4 bg-yellow-200 dark:bg-yellow-900/30 rounded-lg">
<h5 class="font-semibold mb-2 text-yellow-900">The Attention Formula</h5>
<code class="text-sm bg-gray-800 text-green-400 p-2 rounded block">
Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QK^T/d_k)V
</code>
</div>

</div>

<div class="bg-red-200 dark:bg-red-900/20 border-l-4 border-red-600 p-6 mb-8 shadow-sm">

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
<Target class="h-6 w-6 text-red-800 mt-1 flex-shrink-0" />

<div>
<h4 class="font-bold text-red-900 dark:text-red-200 mb-2">Critical Attack Surface</h4>

<div class="text-red-900 dark:text-red-300 mb-3">
The attention mechanism is a primary source of vulnerability. <strong>Attention Hijacking</strong> allows malicious tokens to "grab" disproportionate attention, forming the basis of prompt injection and jailbreaking attacks.
</div>

<div class="bg-red-300 dark:bg-red-800/30 rounded p-3">
<h5 class="font-semibold text-red-900 dark:text-red-200 mb-1">Attack Mechanism</h5>

<div class="text-sm text-red-900 dark:text-red-300">
Attackers craft inputs that manipulate attention patterns to override system instructions, causing the model to focus on adversarial content instead of legitimate constraints.
</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

Effective AI security requires implementing controls across all architectural layers, with each providing independent protection.

<div class="text-green-900 dark:text-green-300 mb-4">
No single control provides complete protection. Layer multiple defenses to create redundancy and resilience.
</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div>
<h4 class="font-semibold text-green-900 dark:text-green-200 mb-2">Input Layer Controls</h4>

<li> Rate limiting and DoS protection</li>
<li> Authentication and authorization</li>
</ul>
